Performance Management

Objective

Maslow is committed to developing a strong learning culture, in which everyone is able to achieve their full potential, and which will increase job satisfaction and support career development. As such, the company is committed to providing excellent training and development opportunities for its staff, which meet the operational and strategic objectives of the organisation, as well as individuals’ own learning aspirations and needs.

This policy and procedure sets out the measures that the company has in place to support its objectives in relation to staff training and development, and the responsibilities that staff at all levels have to ensure that the company develops a strong culture of learning and continuous development.

Scope and Responsibilities

Employees, Head of Divisions and Head of People should all collaborate to build a continuous professional development (CPD) culture. It’s an employee’s responsibility to seek new learning opportunities. It’s a Head of Division responsibility to coach their teams and identify employee development needs. Head of People responsibility to facilitate any staff development activities and Office Management responsible for the process.

Policy
  1. There will be equality of opportunity for all Maslow staff to develop their knowledge, skills and abilities through a blend of learning methods including mentoring, coaching, on the job learning, courses, conferences and seminars.
  2. All training, i.e. courses, workshops, seminars, and conferences will be coordinated through HOD and Head of People.
  3. The training needs of staff will be identified through employee’s performance review and operational planning needs.
  4. Every year the Company will allocate a proportion of the HRDF balance to each division and HOD to use the fund equally for their staff training needs.
  5. Staff attended training to transfer the knowledge/skills learned by shared to other team members.
  6. The Head of Division and Head of People will facilitate the staff member to transfer the knowledge/skills learned in training to the workplace.
Performance Management Cycle
  1. During the Performance review exercise of each financial year, employee together with their superior discuss the training & development needs that required. Once the training and development needs are identified and mutually agreed between the member of staff and Head of Division, submit to People Department for consolidation of employee Training Plan for the year.
  2. The People Department will submit the training plan to HOD for approval and subsequently submit for CEO’s approval and share the approved training plan to staff. The training plan must consist of the following information:
    • Name of Employee, Position, year of service
    • Justification of training required
    • Topic and Contents of Training
    • Tentative Date of training
    • Estimated cost of training
  3. Employee to collaborate with Head of People and HOD to source the suitable training program as per the approved training plan, once the training program is identified and agreeable, HOD & Head of People to approved on the training program.
  4. Employee to submit the following documents to Office Manager for registration at least one (1) month before the training date.
    • Complete registration Form
    • Approved training program by HOD & Head of People that consist of program introduction that indicates the Program name, date, venue, fee and Speaker Profile with IC No.
    • Speaker Profile with IC number
    * Note: The 1month submission required are for the process from registration, confirmation of registration, application and approval of Training Grant from HRDF and payment.
Procedures
  1. Claim must be made in the Expenses Claim Form.
  2. All Claims must be approved by the Head of Division.
  3. The Claim Form must be submitted to Finance Dept. latest by the 20th of each month for reimbursement on the same month
  4. Claim received after the 20th of the month will be reimbursement the next following month.
  5. Claim must be submitted with receipts, claim without receipt will be rejected.
